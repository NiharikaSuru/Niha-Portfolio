Overview

Transforming Healthcare Experience

CarePulse is a patient-centric health portal designed to bridge the gap between patients and healthcare providers. The platform consolidates essential health management features into a single, intuitive interface – making healthcare accessible, understandable, and actionable.

In an era where digital health solutions are becoming increasingly important, CarePulse stands out by focusing on the user's emotional journey through healthcare, not just the functional aspects.

The Challenge

Fragmented Healthcare Experiences

Through initial research and user interviews, I identified several key pain points that patients face when managing their health digitally:

  • Disconnected Systems: Patients juggle multiple apps for appointments, prescriptions, test results, and doctor communication
  • Complex Medical Information: Health data is often presented in clinical terms that patients struggle to understand
  • Appointment Friction: Booking, rescheduling, and preparing for appointments involves too many steps
  • Lack of Personalization: Generic health packages don't consider individual health profiles and needs
  • Poor Vitals Tracking: Patients have no easy way to track trends in their health metrics over time

User research sessions and journey mapping helped uncover key pain points

Solution

Key Features Designed

Based on the research insights, I designed a comprehensive solution that addresses each pain point while maintaining simplicity and clarity.

Personalized Dashboard

Greeting-based interface with health overview, upcoming appointments, and action items at a glance.

Smart Appointment Booking

One-click booking with doctor availability, instant confirmation, and video call integration.

Vitals Tracking

Visual tracking of heart rate, blood pressure, and other metrics with trend analysis and alerts.

Curated Health Packages

Personalized health packages based on age, health profile, and specific needs with clear pricing.

Key screens: Appointment booking and vitals tracking interfaces

Design Decisions

Visual & UX Principles

Every design decision was made with the patient's emotional state in mind. Healthcare can be stressful, so the interface needed to feel calm, trustworthy, and supportive.

  • Calming Color Palette: Soft purples and greens that reduce anxiety while maintaining professionalism
  • Clear Typography: Large, readable text with clear hierarchy – no one should struggle to read their health information
  • Progressive Disclosure: Complex information is revealed gradually, preventing overwhelm
  • Positive Reinforcement: Status indicators use encouraging language ("Normal", "On Track") instead of clinical terms
  • Accessibility First: WCAG 2.1 AA compliance with screen reader support and keyboard navigation

Reflection

Key Learnings

This project reinforced several important principles that I carry into every design challenge:

  • Emotion Matters: In healthcare, how users feel is just as important as what they can do. Design for emotional states, not just tasks.
  • Simplicity Requires Effort: Making complex medical information feel simple required countless iterations and ruthless prioritization.
  • Collaboration is Key: Working closely with healthcare professionals ensured medical accuracy while maintaining user-friendliness.
  • Test Early, Test Often: Early usability testing saved weeks of rework by catching issues before they were deeply implemented.
